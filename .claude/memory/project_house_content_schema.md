Each fashion house entry in the Fashionista project should cover the following fields:

1. Founding year and founder(s)
2. Key milestones in the house's development
3. Current creative director and owner (parent group)
4. Main product lines
5. Distinctive design elements and brand DNA
6. Iconic collections and signature items
7. Market-segment positioning
8. Financial figures or business scale
9. Cultural impact and notable collaborations

**Why:** User defined this content schema on 2026-04-24 as the canonical set of facts to capture for each house. Originally stated in Russian (год основания и основателя, ключевые этапы развития, текущего креативного директора и владельца, основные продуктовые линии, характерные элементы дизайна и ДНК бренда, знаковые коллекции и изделия, позиционирование в сегменте рынка, финансовые показатели или масштаб бизнеса, культурное влияние и коллаборации).

**How to apply:**
- When writing or expanding content about any fashion house (Louis Vuitton, Chanel, Hermès, Gucci, Prada, Dior, Armani, Versace, Balenciaga, Saint Laurent, Fendi, Burberry, Bottega Veneta, Valentino, Givenchy, Dolce & Gabbana, etc.), structure the copy around these nine fields.
- Current short blurbs on the landing page don't yet follow this schema — they are provisional. Per-house pages / detailed cards should.
- Verify currentness of volatile fields (creative director, owner, financials) against fresh sources before committing — creative-director churn is frequent in this industry.
